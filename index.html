Fun Facts

#01

Also called "japanese pizza", there are two main types of okonomiyaki that have developed: Kansai and Hiroshima. They both basically contain the same ingredients but the Hiroshima okonomiyaki also contains soba noodles. We serve the Kansai type!

#02

Originally, the onigiri was used as a way to store left-over rice, but it later became a regular meal.

#03

The word "yaki" that appears in the names of many well-known Japanese dishes(such as yakitori, teriyaki, yakisoba), basically means "cooked over direct heat".

#04

Contrary to popular belief that asian food is very spicy, japanese dishes are generally not spicy at all.

#05

Tofu is an important component of East Asian cuisine and has been consumed in China for over 2.000 years. It was introduced to Japan during the Nara period(710-794).

#06

An essential part of Japanese cuisine is that care is placed into the plating and presentation of the food. It is almost as important as the food itself.
